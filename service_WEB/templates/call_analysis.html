Call Analysis

The AsTeR experience begins with the analysis of emergency calls to 911. We are using AI to understand the calls and process our prioritization algorithm.

During the IBM Call for Code hackathon at UC Berkeley, we decided that the first step of our disaster manager platform will be the analysis of calls to 911 with a prioritization of emergency. The process can be described in few points:

Through speech-to-text technology of IBM Watson, we extract all the words in a conversation with their timing. With time, we are thinking that the conversion in real-time will be used in a lot of call centers and we provide this solution for the emergency service.

We use Natural Language Processing (NLP) models to define a prioritization for the calls. By extracting the main words of each sentence, we already have an idea of the topic and the location. Running our model with these extracting words gives us a precised view of the situation without using the help of an human.

The use of our system will provide an efficient way to compensate the lack of professionals in call centers during a natural disaster. We are thinking that when the amount of calls to 911 will rise in few minutes, our system will provide priorization for high emergency and help the work of the professionals on the ground.
